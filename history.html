2026-06-14 in review NOX-008 — Spatial-scale sensitivity of the NO₂↔steel signal (autocorrelation-robust)

Spec
specs/spatial-scale-sensitivity/
Summary
Sweep two scale axes over the EXISTING cube — AOI extent (0.25 vs 0.10° buffer, clip_cube_to_region) × grid resolution (native + block-averaged 0.10/0.15/0.25°, coarsen_cube; aggregation only, never interpolation) — re-derive the footprint NO₂ signal per scale through the existing pipeline, align vs the CREA BF rate, and judge each correlation with autocorrelation-robust + FDR significance (new noxus/validation/robust.py: first-order + Newey-West effective-N, moving-block bootstrap CI, block-permutation null, Benjamini–Hochberg FDR). Finding
Hardened honest null. Of 128 (scale × variant × lag) tests, 0 are robust after FDR; the 34 naïve-significant cells are all monthly peak-lag artefacts that fall to "fragile (naive-only)". The tight 0.10° AOI does not rescue the signal (Δ|r|≈0.02–0.05, never significant); native↔coarse sign flips are noise-level (|r|<0.13). Conclusion upgraded from "no signal at one scale" to "no robust coupling at any reasonable scale". Also confirmed the naïve Pearson p over-rejects under autocorrelation (probe: every positive "SIG" from the deep exploration collapses).
Decisions
Two recorded in decisions/architecture-decisions.md (2026-06-14): (1) autocorrelation-robust + FDR is the significance standard for index↔benchmark correlations; (2) no robust NO₂↔steel coupling at any reasonable spatial scale.
