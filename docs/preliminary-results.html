NOXUS · NOX-003 · preliminary result

Does the Tangshan steel cluster's satellite NO₂ track blast-furnace activity?

A first real, end-to-end run of the relative-index pipeline (TROPOMI NO₂ → steel-facility footprint → background subtraction → ERA5 meteo control → deseasonalisation → relative index) validated against the CREA blast-furnace operating rate, 2019–2026. The headline: the raw signal is dominated by a policy-driven decline, and only a weak, below-bar activity signal survives detrending.

Generated 2026-06-13 · footprint radius 5 km (84 cells) · weekly cube (389 wks) · ERA5 via Copernicus CDS · benchmark CREA BF operating rate. Reproduce: uv run python analysis/preliminary_signal.py. Preliminary — not the final NOX-003 result.

One-sentence conclusion

In Tangshan, satellite NO₂ over the steel cluster is dominated by a policy/technology-driven decline (NO₂ falls while the blast-furnace rate holds — the Li 2024 confounder); after removing that secular trend a faint, correctly-signed activity signal appears (monthly year-over-year r ≈ 0.19, peak +0.29 at lag +6 mo) but stays below the literature bar (0.5–0.75). This is an honest, mechanistically-explained near-null — a valid project outcome (Montgomery 2018; Morris & Zhang 2019), not a pipeline failure.

1 · The raw signal has the wrong sign — a secular decoupling

At the level (no detrending), the footprint NO₂ and the BF operating rate are negatively correlated (weekly r = −0.20, monthly r = −0.35, both significant). NO₂ declines across 2019–2026 while the BF rate holds: the ultra-low-emission retrofit of Chinese steel cut NO₂ per unit of output, decoupling the column from production exactly as Li 2024 documented for China's 2020–2022 NOₓ decline. This is a confounder, not inverse causation.

2 · Detrending flips the sign positive — but only faintly

Removing the annual cycle and secular trend with a year-over-year change recovers the expected positive co-movement. It is weak and monthly > weekly (Morris & Zhang: weekly is noisy).

3 · The deseasonalisation method is decisive

The first attempt used aggressive double-differencing and saw a flat null — an artefact, exactly as Kim 2023 warned (they used seasonal adjustment, not double-diff). Year-over-year and STL preserve the faint signal; double-diff erases it. The default was changed from yoy-double-diff to yoy.

4 · Footprint radius: tighter is better

The signal is strongest at a tight footprint (5 km, 84 cells, r ≈ 0.19) and decays to ≈ 0 by 15 km (448 cells) as background dilutes the steel term — consistent with the Kim/Parubets warning that source isolation, not maximal coverage, matters. (The 15 km placeholder used in the very first run was diluting the signal.)

5 · Modelling the intensity decline explicitly (NOX-003.1)

Year-over-year differencing removes the retrofit trend blindly, and double-differencing over-removes it (Fig 5). NOX-003.1 instead models the secular emission-intensity decline explicitly: it fits a smoothness-controlled trend s(t) to the footprint signal and takes the residual as the activity proxy — the Li 2024 activity / intensity decomposition. The trend smoothness (effective degrees of freedom) is chosen by cross-validation on the NO₂ series alone; the benchmark is never consulted, so the detrending cannot be tuned to manufacture a correlation (the central anti-p-hacking guard).

The result confirms the decoupling and reproduces the yoy finding without the arbitrariness: CV picks a near-linear trend (df = 2), and the residual tracks the BF rate at monthly r ≈ 0.14 (peak ≈ 0.19) — comparable to yoy (0.19), still below the 0.5–0.75 bar. The raw levels stay at r = −0.35.

The smoothness-sensitivity sweep is the honesty check: the residual correlation is highest at minimal detrending (df = 2) and decays monotonically toward zero as the trend grows wigglier (df = 10) — the over- vs under-smoothing trade-off, with the CV-selected df marked. There is no robust high-df plateau: the activity signal is real but weak and lives in the low-frequency band the trend competes for, and no df choice manufactures a spurious correlation.

The full-series re-run (REQ-120/121) remains the go/no-go test for whether a longer sample clears the bar.

How this sits against the literature

  • Li 2024 — China's 2020–2022 NOₓ decline was policy-led, not activity-led. Our negative level correlation is the same signature: retrofit-driven NO₂ decline decoupled from output.
  • Kim 2023 — raw column correlation is insignificant; signal needs source isolation + seasonal adjustment; r ≈ 0.55–0.71 for a clean sector link. We confirm the raw/double-diff null and the detrending sensitivity, but land below their band.
  • Wen 2024 — steel is only 30–43 % of Tangshan NO₂ (and seasonally varying); a hard cap on how much of the column is attributable.
  • Montgomery 2018 / Morris & Zhang 2019 — the link is not universal and results are heterogeneous; reporting a well-explained null is legitimate.

6 · What could still move the needle (next)

  • Full-series re-run (NOX-003.1 REQ-120/121) — the cube here is partial; a longer sample is the real test of whether the explicit-intensity residual clears the bar, and the gate for whether NOX-005 is worth it.
  • Change-point / segmented intensity trend — the smooth s(t) mis-fits discrete retrofit step-changes; a segmented trend may sharpen the residual further.
  • Flux-divergence point-source attribution (Beirle; deferred NOX-005) to isolate steel emitters from the >50 % non-steel background the footprint−ring still mixes in.
  • Confirm robustness with an explicit curtailment calendar (Q6a).

Reproducibility

Every execution mode is preserved and config-selectable (deseason ∈ {yoy, stl, yoy-double-diff, intensity-model, none}; frequency ∈ {weekly, monthly}; meteo on/off; footprint radius; trend smoothness grid). Regenerate this entire page's figures + tables:

uv run noxus ingest-benchmark      # CREA BF operating rate -> parquet
uv run noxus grid                  # TROPOMI NO2 weekly cube
uv run noxus ingest-era5           # ERA5 (Copernicus CDS, server-side AOI subset)
uv run python analysis/preliminary_signal.py   # battery + figures -> docs/figures/preliminary/

Inputs (NO₂ cube, ERA5 snapshot, derived index) are gitignored; the figures, tables and this page are committed as the publishable result.
