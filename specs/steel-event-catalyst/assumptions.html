Assumptions — Steel NO₂ event catalyst

Task IDNOX-004
Feature slugsteel-event-catalyst
Created2026-06-13

Purpose

Explicit assumptions behind the catalyst/event-marker paradigm. A1 is the load-bearing one — if production events are not detectable as NO₂ deviations, the whole approach reduces to a reported null (which is acceptable).

Assumptions

A1Production events leave a detectable NO₂ footprint signatureLoad-bearing
Assumption
Discrete steel-production events (curtailment start/end, sudden shutdowns/restarts) produce NO₂ deviations over the footprint that are large relative to noise after intensity-detrending + meteo normalisation.
Why it matters
This is what makes the marker work. Catalysts live in the tails; if events don't move the residual beyond the noise band, there is nothing to detect.
If false
Precision/recall are low and the catalyst is a reported null — a valid outcome (NFR-004), and a cleaner result than the weak continuous correlation.
A2The decoupling trend does not create sudden jumpsSupported
Assumption
The secular emission-intensity decline (retrofits, Li 2024) is slow/smooth, so it does not generate the step changes the detector looks for; it is removed once via the NOX-003.1 residual.
Why it matters
The trend that killed the continuous correlation is therefore not a confounder for event detection.
If false
A sharp retrofit step (EDGE-103 of NOX-003.1) could be mis-detected as a drop event; the cause-tagging + calendar help separate policy from technology.
A3Meteorology is the dominant false-positive source on short windowsMethod
Assumption
On weekly/event timescales a stagnant-air week is the main way NO₂ spikes without a production change; the strengthened ventilation/stagnation control (REQ-005) removes most of it.
Why it matters
Event precision depends on rejecting weather spikes; this is why the cheap meteo upgrade — not flux-divergence — is the right lever.
If false
Residual meteo leakage inflates false positives; reported via the precision metric and the multiplicity discipline.
A4A best-effort public curtailment calendar is assemblableData
Assumption
Enough Tangshan curtailment episodes are publicly documented (MEE/CREA) to label production events; combined with BF-rate jumps (developer decision: both).
Why it matters
Ground truth quality bounds recall and cause-tagging.
If false
Fall back to BF-rate jumps alone and document the calendar gap (NOX-003 Q6a); recall is then defined against the benchmark only.
A5Free market data is sufficient for the headline studyData
Assumption
Global miners (BHP/RIO/VALE) + a steel ETF, from a free source, are enough to test the market hypothesis; Chinese ferrous futures are a desirable add-on, not a prerequisite.
Why it matters
Keeps the project's public/free-data constraint intact while still testing the catalyst.
If false
If only the ferrous futures carry the relationship and they are inaccessible, the market layer reports a null on the free instruments and flags ferrous as deferred (Q1).
A6The tradeable edge is timeliness / gap-filling, not resolutionFraming
Assumption
The operating rate is already published weekly, so any edge comes from NO₂ being timelier than the vendor print or flagging events the survey smooths — not from finer resolution.
Why it matters
Sets the success criterion: a positive lead vs publication, not merely a correlation with an already-public number.
If false
If NO₂ does not lead the print, the market study is a null even if events are detectable — reported honestly.
