Summary

Derive a relative steel-sector activity index for the Tangshan cluster from the analysis-ready weekly NO₂ cube (NOX-002b) and test, transparently, whether it tracks / leads the CREA blast-furnace operating-rate benchmark (NOX-001). This task revives the validation/correlation module that was reverted to a scaffold in NOX-001 (there was no predictor to validate against yet) and reconciles its design with the new attribution stage.

Paradigm (locked, developer-approved 2026-06-13): v1 is the relative-index / cluster-attribution blueprint (Kim 2023) — sample a footprint around the steel facilities, subtract a regional background, normalise meteorology, deseasonalise, emit a relative index, correlate against throughput. Flux-divergence point-source attribution (Beirle 2021/2023) is explicitly out of scope and deferred to NOX-005 (it needs a raw L2 swath path and is known to resolve poorly in high-background / complex-wind regions like industrial NE China).

The pipeline is: weekly cube → sample on the facility footprint → subtract/normalise a regional background → meteorological normalisation (ERA5 wind/PBL regress-out) → deseasonalise → relative activity index → validate vs CREA (Pearson r + p-values, lead/lag) → report. A rigorous, honest NULL is an acceptable, designed-for outcome (Morris & Zhang 2019).

New data dependency: ERA5 reanalysis (wind + boundary-layer height) ingested from the Copernicus CDS (server-side AOI subset), consistent with the project's public-data-only constraint.

Functional requirements

EARS format. IDs are stable references for tasks and tests. Each requirement notes its rationale and the literature that motivates it.

Footprint sampling & background

REQ-001When the signal pipeline runs, the system shall load the weekly NO₂ cube from NOX-002b (data/derived/no2/no2_cube_w.nc; vars no2, coverage) and the operating facilities from data/derived/tangshan_steel_facilities.csv, filtering to facilities whose status indicates active operation (default: operating and operating pre-retirement; retired/cancelled excluded).
REQ-002When sampling the signal, the system shall extract cells on a configurable footprint around the operating facility locations (a radius/buffer in km, default to be confirmed — see open questions) and aggregate them into a per-period footprint NO₂ value over valid cells, rather than a flat AOI mean. Rationale: a flat AOI mean dilutes the cluster signal with background; sampling the footprint follows the Kim (2023) "isolate the source from background" blueprint.
REQ-003When constructing the signal, the system shall estimate a regional background from a configurable geometry (default: an annular ring outside the facility footprint but inside the AOI) and shall subtract and/or normalise the footprint value against it, producing a background-corrected footprint signal. Rationale: background subtraction is required to separate the cluster term from the BTH regional NO₂ field (Kim 2023; trap 4).
REQ-004While sampling and background-correcting, the system shall propagate the NOX-002b per-period valid-coverage and apply the inherited minimum-coverage screening; periods/cells below threshold are marked missing and never interpolated (NOX-002b cloud decision, 2026-06-13).

Meteorological normalisation (ERA5)

REQ-010When meteorological normalisation is enabled, the system shall ingest ERA5 wind components and boundary-layer height (and any further configured variables) over the AOI and era (2019→present) from the public Copernicus Climate Data Store, persisting a dated/versioned local snapshot so analysis is reproducible from the snapshot (no live fetch at analysis time).
REQ-011When normalising the signal, the system shall regress the meteorological covariates out of the background-corrected footprint signal (Mao 2025 / Li 2024 approach), so the index reflects activity net of the dominant weekly ventilation confounder; the regression form (linear residualisation vs LOESS-style filtering) shall be configurable and recorded in the run output. Rationale: wind/PBL drive most of the week-to-week NO₂ column variance; not removing them lets weather masquerade as activity.

Deseasonalisation & confounder modelling

REQ-020When deseasonalising, the system shall remove seasonality from the signal and benchmark by a configurable, recorded method (default: yoy year-over-year change, resolved 2026-06-13 — double-differencing erased the signal on this series, Q4; alternatives: stl, yoy-double-diff, none). Rationale: raw VCD correlations are insignificant; deseasonalisation is decisive for a clean sector link (Kim 2023; Kondragunta 2021).
REQ-021When modelling, the system shall explicitly model the heating/non-heating seasonal confounder as a structural term (e.g. a heating-season indicator or season-specific scaling), not merely deseasonalise it away, because the steel share of Tangshan NO₂ itself shifts from 42.5% (non-heating) to 29.1% (heating) (Wen et al. 2024, Scientific Reports) — the sensitivity of the column to steel activity is season-dependent, so a single deseasonalisation mis-specifies it.
REQ-022When modelling, the system shall include known policy/production-curtailment periods as exogenous controls, because emission controls can cut NOₓ while physical output rises (Li et al. 2024); without this control the indicator can invert.

Relative activity index

REQ-030When all corrections are applied, the system shall emit a relative activity index (a normalised, unitless series; e.g. indexed to an anchor period or z-scored), and shall not claim an absolute steel-tonnage figure. Rationale: (a) TROPOMI underestimates the column by 23–33% in polluted air (Kondragunta 2021), and (b) steel is only ~30–43% of Tangshan NO₂ (Wen et al. 2024), so the pixel→tonnage map is non-linear and uncalibrated — only a relative signal is defensible.
REQ-031When the index is emitted, the system shall record the configured attributable-fraction cap (~30–43%, Wen et al. 2024) in the run provenance as the ceiling on how much of the column variation can plausibly be steel, and shall surface it in the report so the index is not over-interpreted.

Validation vs CREA

REQ-040When validation runs, the system shall align the relative index and the CREA blast-furnace operating-rate benchmark (data/derived/benchmark_tangshan_bf_operating_rate.parquet) to a common frequency (default weekly) using a configured, documented aggregation rule, honouring the coverage screening.
REQ-041When validation runs, the system shall empirically verify the sign of the index↔benchmark relationship for Tangshan and report it before claiming a usable signal, because the NO₂↔activity sign is income/region dependent and not fixed (Montgomery & Holloway 2018). A negative or insignificant sign is a reportable outcome, not a failure to suppress.
REQ-042When validation runs, the system shall compute the Pearson correlation r with its p-value between the deseasonalised index and benchmark, and a cross-correlation / lead-lag profile over a configured lag window, reporting the peak-correlation lag with confidence bounds.
REQ-043When validation completes, the system shall emit a machine-readable results artifact and a human-readable summary that states the conclusion in plain language — including, explicitly, the NULL case (no usable lead/correlation after controls) as a valid, designed-for result (Morris & Zhang 2019), and shall report effect sizes with confidence intervals rather than a bare point estimate.
REQ-044When reporting, the system shall state the result against the literature success bar of r ≈ 0.5–0.75 on the deseasonalised series (Kim 2023 / Kondragunta 2021 band), classifying the outcome relative to that band.

CLI / orchestration

REQ-050When the user runs the signal and validation via the noxus CLI, the system shall expose them as documented subcommands driven by noxus/config/ (footprint radius, background geometry, ERA5 variables + regression form, deseasonalisation method, lag window, anchor/index base, attributable-fraction cap, paths), reusing/extending the existing attribute, index, and validate placeholder subcommands.

Non-functional requirements

NFR-001Reproducibility. Every reported result shall be reproducible from the committed cube + benchmark parquet + ERA5 snapshot + recorded config; no live fetch at analysis time.
NFR-002Public/free data only. ERA5 is ingested from the free Copernicus CDS; no commercial/paywalled inputs.
NFR-003Honest reporting. The summary shall present r/p with confidence intervals and the sign check; it shall not present an unconditioned in-sample correlation as proof of a leading indicator, and shall report the null when present.
NFR-004No-publish. NO₂-derived outputs (footprint signal, index, ERA5-derived fields over the AOI) are gitignored, consistent with the NOX-002b no-publish-downloaded-data decision; the small results summary/CREA-aligned artifact may be committed if it contains no raw NO₂ field.
NFR-005Determinism. Given identical inputs and config, outputs shall be deterministic (fixed seeds where any randomness exists).
NFR-006Stack conformance. Python ≥3.12; numpy/pandas/xarray/pyarrow (+ any approved stats dependency); passes uv run pytest and uv run ruff check ..

Edge cases

EDGE-001Two facilities share identical coordinates (e.g. the Caofeidian rows at 39.160401, 118.429806) → footprint cells are de-duplicated, not double-counted.
EDGE-002The footprint and background ring overlap, or the background ring leaves the AOI → the geometry is clipped to the cube extent and the system reports the effective cell counts; an empty background ring is an error (ERR-003).
EDGE-003Heavy-cloud weeks fall below the inherited coverage threshold → footprint/background marked missing for that week, not interpolated; the validation aligns on the surviving overlap.
EDGE-004Short overlapping sample (TROPOMI 2019→present vs weekly CREA) limits lag orders → cap lags to keep degrees of freedom positive and report the cap (cf. NOX-001 EDGE-005).
EDGE-005Mixed TROPOMI processor versions (v1/v2 discontinuity, carried in NOX-002b provenance) → surfaced and, where feasible, controlled (e.g. version indicator) so a reprocessing step is not mistaken for an activity change.
EDGE-006ERA5 grid/cadence differs from the NO₂ grid → ERA5 is regridded/aggregated to the composite frequency and footprint before regress-out; the alignment rule is recorded.
EDGE-007Sign verification (REQ-041) returns negative/insignificant → pipeline still completes and reports the null; it does not silently flip the sign to force a positive result.

Error states

ERR-001If the NOX-002b cube or the NOX-001 benchmark parquet is missing, the system shall fail with an actionable message naming the missing artifact and the command that produces it ("run 'noxus grid' / 'noxus ingest-benchmark' first").
ERR-002If the ERA5 snapshot is missing or unreadable while meteorological normalisation is enabled, the system shall fail with an actionable message (how to fetch ERA5), and shall not silently skip the meteo control.
ERR-003If no facility falls inside the cube extent, or the background ring contains no valid cells, the system shall raise a descriptive geometry error rather than emit an empty/degenerate signal.
ERR-004If, after alignment and coverage screening, the overlapping index/benchmark sample is shorter than a configured minimum length, the system shall refuse to run the statistical tests and report insufficient data rather than emit unstable estimates (cf. NOX-001 ERR-003).

Acceptance criteria

AC-001Given a synthetic cube + facility CSV, footprint sampling selects exactly the cells within the configured radius of operating facilities (retired/cancelled excluded; co-located dups handled) and the per-period footprint value equals the manual mean over valid footprint cells.
AC-002Background subtraction/normalisation yields the manually-computed background-corrected signal; an empty background ring raises ERR-003.
AC-003With a synthetic ERA5 field, meteo regress-out removes the planted wind/PBL component from the signal (residual is meteorology-free to tolerance); the regression form is recorded.
AC-004Deseasonalisation removes a planted seasonal term; the heating/non-heating term and curtailment controls are present and recorded in the run output.
AC-005The relative index is unitless/normalised with the attributable-fraction cap recorded; no absolute-tonnage value is emitted.
AC-006On a planted-signal synthetic dataset (benchmark = f(index at lag k) + noise) validation recovers sign, lag k, and r in band; on a planted-null dataset (independent series) it reports the null with r near zero and a non-significant p — proving the pipeline reports the null honestly.
AC-007The summary states the conclusion in plain language including the null, reports r/p with CIs and the sign check, classifies against the r≈0.5–0.75 bar, and lists the deseasonalisation method, meteo covariates, curtailment controls, footprint/background geometry, lag window, and attributable-fraction cap used.
AC-008uv run pytest and uv run ruff check . pass.
