Assumptions — Spatial-scale sensitivity of the NO₂↔steel signal

Task IDNOX-008
Feature slugspatial-scale-sensitivity
Created2026-06-14

Purpose

Explicit assumptions behind the scale sweep. A1 (the resolution analogy) is load-bearing for how the result is interpreted; if it is misread, the Parubets comparison is over-claimed.

Assumptions

A1"Resolution" here ≈ within-AOI averaging coarseness, not Parubets' panelLoad-bearing
Assumption
Block-averaging the cube before the footprint/background contrast is a defensible analogue of Parubets' choice of GDP-grid resolution; both change how much non-target area is blended into the signal.
Why it matters
Parubets ran a cross-prefecture two-way FE panel; we run a single AOI-mean/footprint time series. The mechanisms overlap (aggregation scale) but are not identical.
If false
The coarse↔fine difference is still a valid sensitivity result for our estimator; we report it as background-dilution sensitivity and soften the "replicates Parubets" claim. Stated in the report (REQ-041).
A2Block-averaging is the right coarsening operatorMethod
Assumption
A simple mean over non-overlapping native-cell blocks (coverage-weighted, NaN-aware) approximates a coarser native grid well enough; we do not need area-weighting or a re-projection.
Why it matters
The cube is on a near-regular lon/lat grid over a small AOI, so equal-weight block means are close to area means.
If false
Add coverage/area weights to coarsen_cube; the interface already carries coverage. Low risk over this small AOI.
A3First-order n_eff is a usable floor; Newey-West is the refinementMethod
Assumption
The Bayley–Hammersley first-order effective-N is a reasonable, dependency-free lower-bound correction; the full-order Newey-West estimate sharpens it for series with seasonal/high-order autocorrelation (e.g. the level).
Why it matters
The 2026-06-14 probe used first-order only and noted it under-corrects the highly-autocorrelated level; the full-order estimate closes that gap.
If false
The block-permutation p (which makes no parametric assumption) is the arbiter; n_eff is reported alongside, not alone.
A4The committed cube is the right substrateVerified
Assumption
The full-series cube (389 weeks, rebuilt for NOX-003.1 T10) is complete enough that the sweep is the decisive test, not a partial-data artefact.
Why it matters
A partial cube would confound "scale effect" with "missing weeks".
If false
Label the run partial (EDGE-005); the cube-completeness gate (NOX-003.1 ERR-103) already exists. Checked 2026-06-14: cube spans 30×51 cells, wide AOI; tight AOI is a strict subset.
A5Coarsening cannot create signalHonesty
Assumption
Averaging cells removes information; any apparent improvement at a coarser/tighter scale is reduced background dilution (better SNR for the steel term), not new signal.
Why it matters
Sets honest expectations and frames the report; prevents over-claiming a "scale that works".
If false
n/a — this is a statement of the physics, enforced in the report wording (NFR-007).
A6The honest-null discipline carries overInherited
Assumption
A null across all scales is an acceptable, publishable outcome (Morris & Zhang 2019), as in NOX-003/003.1/004/006.
Why it matters
The task is designed to be informative whether or not a scale rescues the signal.
If false
n/a.
