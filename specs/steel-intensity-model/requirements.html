Summary

NOX-003 produced an honest, two-layer result: in levels the footprint NO₂ correlates negatively with the CREA blast-furnace operating rate (monthly r ≈ −0.35); after blind year-over-year detrending it turns weakly positive (monthly r ≈ 0.19, peak +0.29 at lag +6 months), below the literature bar (r ≈ 0.5–0.75). The negative-levels relationship is not noise — it is the signature of decoupling: emission intensity (NOₓ per tonne of steel) falls secularly faster than activity rises, driven by retrofits/policy (Li et al. 2024). The blind filters currently available (yoy, yoy-double-diff) either under-remove that trend (leaving the negative bias) or over-remove it (double-diff erased the signal on the weekly series, Q4 of NOX-003).

This task adds an explicit emission-intensity model: instead of differencing blindly, fit a smoothness-controlled secular intensity trend s(t) on the meteorology-normalised footprint signal and define the activity proxy as the residual after removing s(t) — the Li-2024 activity / intensity / meteorology decomposition. The smoothness is selected by an out-of-sample / cross-validation criterion (never tuned against the benchmark), and a smoothness-sensitivity sweep is reported so the reader can see whether any signal is an artefact of one smoothing choice. The secular trend itself is emitted as a reportable diagnostic (the decoupling), not silently discarded.

The task also performs the deferred full-series re-run: once the complete TROPOMI fetch + rebuilt cube exist, re-run the whole chain on the longer sample (current cube is partial; n is too short to trust r ≈ 0.19) and (re)tune the footprint radius and coverage thresholds on the complete coverage distribution — resolving NOX-003 open questions Q1/Q5 and NOX-002b Q2.

Locked scope (inherited, not re-opened): relative-index / cluster paradigm (Kim 2023); flux-divergence (Beirle) stays deferred to NOX-005. No code is removed: all existing execution modes (yoy / stl / yoy-double-diff / none) remain selectable for replicability; the intensity model is an additive new method. A rigorous NULL remains an acceptable, designed-for outcome (Morris & Zhang 2019).

Functional requirements

EARS format. IDs are stable references for tasks and tests. Numbering continues from NOX-003 in the 1xx range to avoid collision.

Explicit emission-intensity (decoupling) model

REQ-101When the intensity-model detrending method is selected, the system shall fit an explicit secular emission-intensity trend s(t) on the background-corrected, meteorology-normalised footprint signal using a smooth estimator (penalised regression spline by default; LOESS available), and shall define the activity proxy as the residual signal − s(t). Rationale: levels are dominated by the secular intensity decline (decoupling, Li 2024); a blind difference filter (yoy/yoy-double-diff) over- or under-removes that trend.
REQ-102When fitting s(t), the system shall select the trend smoothness by an out-of-sample / cross-validation criterion (e.g. blocked time-series CV or GCV) and shall never select smoothness by maximising correlation with the benchmark; the selected smoothness (effective degrees of freedom) and the selection criterion shall be recorded in the run provenance. Rationale: the smoothness knob is the dominant researcher degree of freedom here; tuning it against the validation target would manufacture a correlation (Morris & Zhang 2019).
REQ-103When the intensity model runs, the system shall produce a smoothness-sensitivity sweep: the activity↔benchmark correlation (and peak lead-lag) as a function of trend effective degrees of freedom across a configured range, so the reader can see whether the signal survives across smoothing choices or is an artefact of one. Rationale: the secular trend and the activity term partly share timescales — double-differencing erased the signal — so the separation must be shown to be robust, not assumed.
REQ-104When the intensity model runs, the system shall expose the additive decomposition in the Li-2024 frame — meteorology (already regressed out, REQ-011), the secular intensity trend s(t), and the activity residual — and shall emit s(t) as a reportable diagnostic series (the decoupling itself), not merely discard it. Rationale: the negative levels↔activity relationship is a finding, not noise to suppress.

Decoupling reporting

REQ-110When reporting, the system shall state explicitly both the levels↔benchmark relationship (expected negative — the intensity decline) and the residual (activity)↔benchmark relationship, each quantified with r, p and confidence interval, framing the negative levels relationship as the retrofit/decoupling signal (Li 2024) rather than as a failed positive correlation.

Full-series re-run & tuning

REQ-120When the complete TROPOMI fetch and the rebuilt NOX-002b cube are available, the system shall re-run the full chain (footprint → background → meteo → intensity model → validation) on the complete weekly series (and the monthly robustness view) and report the result on the longer sample. Rationale: the current cube is partial (232 slots / 76 aligned rows); n is too short to trust the r ≈ 0.19 estimate or its lag.
REQ-121When re-running, the system shall (re)tune the footprint radius (NOX-003 Q1) and the per-period / per-cell coverage thresholds (NOX-003 Q5, NOX-002b Q2) on the complete coverage distribution, recording the chosen values and emitting a sensitivity table (r vs radius; r vs coverage threshold). This resolves those inherited open questions on real data.

Existing subcommands and their defaults shall not break; the previous default behaviour remains reproducible.

Non-functional requirements

NFR-101Reproducibility. Every reported result reproducible from the committed cube + benchmark + ERA5 snapshot + recorded config (incl. the selected smoothness); no live fetch at analysis time.
NFR-102No p-hacking. The smoothness selection rule is fixed before looking at the benchmark correlation; the full config and the sensitivity sweep are recorded so the result is auditable (Morris & Zhang 2019).
NFR-103Backward compatibility. Existing deseason methods and CLI behaviour are unchanged; the intensity model is additive.
NFR-104No-publish. NO₂-derived outputs (footprint signal, index, trend/residual series) remain gitignored; only the small stats summary + figures may be committed.
NFR-105Determinism. Given identical inputs/config the decomposition, CV split and outputs are deterministic (fixed seeds/folds).
NFR-106Stack conformance. Python ≥3.12; reuse existing deps (numpy/pandas/xarray/scipy/statsmodels); passes uv run pytest and uv run ruff check .. No new heavy dependency unless approved (open question).

Edge cases

EDGE-101Series too short for a stable trend fit / CV (e.g. partial cube) → the intensity model refuses to over-fit and either falls back to a recorded low-df trend or raises ERR-101, rather than fitting a wiggly trend that absorbs the activity.
EDGE-102Over-smoothing limit (df→1, near-linear trend) vs under-smoothing limit (df large, trend absorbs activity) → both ends appear in the sensitivity sweep (REQ-103) so the trade-off is visible; the CV-selected point is marked.
EDGE-103The retrofit decline is a step change (discrete plant upgrade) rather than smooth → a single smooth trend mis-fits near the step; flagged via residual diagnostics; change-point handling is noted as future work, not v3.1 scope.
EDGE-104Heavy-cloud gaps in the longer series → trend fit handles missing periods (gap-aware), coverage screening still applies, no interpolation across gaps for validation alignment.
EDGE-105Full re-run requested but the complete cube is not yet present → the system runs on the available partial series and clearly labels the output as partial (REQ-120 acceptance gate deferred), rather than silently claiming a full-series result.

Error states

ERR-101If the intensity-model method is selected but the series is shorter than a configured minimum for a stable trend fit + CV, the system shall raise an actionable error (state the minimum and the available length) rather than emit an over-fit trend.
ERR-102If the smoothness range / CV configuration is degenerate (e.g. empty df grid, folds > samples), the system shall fail with a descriptive configuration error.
ERR-103If the full re-run (REQ-120) is invoked with an explicit "require complete cube" flag but the cube is incomplete, the system shall refuse and name the command that completes the fetch, rather than report a partial result as full.

Acceptance criteria

AC-101On a planted-decomposition synthetic series (signal = activity + known smooth trend + β·meteo + noise) the intensity model recovers the planted trend within tolerance, the residual correlates with the planted activity, and — when the trend dominates — the levels correlate negatively with activity while the residual correlates positively. This proves the decoupling mechanism is modelled, not assumed.
AC-102The smoothness CV selects a degrees-of-freedom near the planted-trend smoothness on synthetic data, and the selection does not consult the benchmark; the chosen df + criterion are recorded.
AC-103The smoothness-sensitivity sweep (REQ-103) is produced as a table/figure across the df range with the CV-selected point marked; on a planted-null series the sweep stays near r≈0 across all df (no df choice manufactures a correlation).
AC-104The decomposition emits the secular trend s(t) as a diagnostic series and the report states both the negative levels relationship and the residual relationship with r/p/CI (REQ-104, REQ-110).
AC-105Existing deseason methods (yoy/stl/yoy-double-diff/none) and CLI behaviour are unchanged (regression); the intensity model is selectable additively (REQ-140, NFR-103).
AC-106Full re-run (data-gated): when the complete cube exists, the chain runs on the longer series and the footprint-radius / coverage-threshold sensitivity tables are emitted (REQ-120/121). If the cube is still partial at implementation time, this AC is satisfied by the partial-labelled run plus the tuning harness, with the full-series number recorded as a follow-up.
AC-107The analysis battery + result note are extended with the trend/residual, sensitivity and sign figures (REQ-130) and all execution modes remain runnable from the same script.
AC-108uv run pytest and uv run ruff check . pass.
